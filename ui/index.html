How it works, and its honest limit

Multi-adversarial sanitization

An encoder maps candidate features to a latent z. A hiring predictor learns from z; simultaneously, gender and age auditors attack z through a Gradient Reversal Layer. The reversed gradient pushes the encoder to make z useless to the auditors while keeping it useful for hiring — running multiple adversaries at once is the contribution over a textbook single-adversary setup.

What we will not claim

Adversarial training enforces representational invariance on the data manifold — gender becomes undecodable from z and group-fairness gaps shrink. It does not guarantee a counterfactually invariant score: perturb a candidate along a gender proxy and the sanitized model actually reacts more, not less. We report this rather than hide it. Closing that gap — a counterfactual-consistency objective — is the natural next step.
